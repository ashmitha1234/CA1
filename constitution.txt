The Constitution of India is the supreme law of the country. It defines the structure, powers, and duties of the government, as well as the rights and duties of citizens. 
"Key features of the constitution"
The Constitution was adopted by the Constituent Assembly on November 26, 1949 and came into force on January 26, 1950. 
It guarantees fundamental rights to all citizens. 
It establishes a parliamentary system of government. 
It binds the legislative, executive, and judicial organs of government. 
It empowers the judiciary to invalidate laws or government actions that violate the Constitution.

"Fundamental rights" 
Right to equality
Right to freedom of speech and expression
Right to freedom of conscience
Right to practice any profession or occupation
Right against exploitation
Right to conserve culture, language, or script

Chief architect of our constitution:
Dr. B. R. Ambedkar, who chaired the Drafting Committee, is considered the chief architect of the Constitution.

"Purpose"
The Constitution provides a framework to guide and govern the country, keeping in view its social, cultural, and religious diversity.

Many ammendments have been made to the constitution based on the situation.

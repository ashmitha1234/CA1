NDRF(National Disaster Response Force)

NDRF stands for National Disaster Response Force, a specialized force in India that responds to natural and man-made disasters. The Government of India founded NDRF in 2006.

How does NDRF work?

  • NDRF is the world's largest disaster response force.
  • NDRF has saved over 155,000 lives and evacuated more than 800,000 people from disaster-stricken areas.
  • NDRF has been praised for its rapid and efficient response to major disasters like the 2011 Japan Triple Disaster, the 2015 Nepal Earthquake, and the 2023 Türkiye Earthquake

NDRF's organization

  • NDRF is organized on para-military lines.
  • Each battalion of NDRF has about 1,149 personnel.
  • Each battalion has 18 search and rescue teams of 45 personnel each.
  • These teams include engineers, technicians, electricians, dog squads, and medical and paramedics.

NDRF's personnel

  • NDRF personnel are on deputation from the para-military forces of India.
  • These forces include the Border Security Force, Central Reserve Police Force, and more.
